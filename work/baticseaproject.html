The Baltic Sea Project

the past, present and visions of the baltic sea

SCROLL

DOCUMENTATION

The Baltic Sea project is an initiative by the Rotary Club of Kalmar to raise awareness on the best practices at sea and engage the young to participate in imagining the future of the Baltic Sea. The exhibition included a wide array of illustrations, children's books, scientific research and infographics, and interactive installations aimed at parents and children.


I worked as a part of the curatorial team, as the illustrator for the period 1955-1999 in the illustration series and as the interactive sensory installation designer for "Guess the Scrap" station.


The exhibition travels to different places every summer, it was Exhibited at Giraffen Mall, Kalmar in 2024, the Public City Library in Mnsters in 2025 and potentially Oskarshamn next year.

RESPONSES FROM "GUESS THE SCRAP" STATION

"Guess the Scrap" station was particularly popular among the children who guessed common items that is tossed at sea. The sensory and interactive aspects of the installation proved to be very engaging and memorable for the children
